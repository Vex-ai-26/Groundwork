## Top-Selling Formats & Features

### **Format Preferences:**

**1. Excel/Google Sheets (WINNER)** 🏆
- 
Estimate templates are fully-customizable and accessible through Excel or Google Sheets which makes them easy to incorporate into existing preconstruction workflows

- Automatic calculations essential for contractors
- Most professional and functional

**2. PDF (SECONDARY)**
- 
Available in both Word and PDF formats, allowing to edit, save, and print as required

- Good for printable forms, less for calculations

**3. Canva (NOT RECOMMENDED)**
- 
Canva has templates and design tools that can mimic a Gantt chart, but lack the features that project managers need. Design apps like Canva aren't built for that sort of work

- Not suitable for construction calculation needs

### **Essential Features Contractors Need:**

**Core Components:**
- 
Labor, materials, equipment, overhead, and profit in an organized, professional format

- 
Columns for construction categories, unit quantities, price per unit, base prices, markup amount, and profit

- 
Overhead and profit percentages in calculation cells with automated calculations for totals, subtotals, and tax calculations


**Labor/Materials Breakdown:**
- 
Dedicated columns for labor and materials with automatic calculation of cost per square foot and total costs

- 
Supply, material, and labor are the main components. Detailed estimate that lists each of the materials required


**Markup Calculators:**
- 
The construction industry standard is the 10-10 rule, meaning markup 10% for profit and 10% for overhead

- Automatic profit margin calculations

**Additional Must-Haves:**
- 
Itemized materials with quantities and unit pricing, labor costs by task or phase, subcontractor and equipment expenses, permit fees and miscellaneous costs, overhead allocation, markup and profit calculation

- Client information fields
- Project scope description
- Signature sections for approval
- Tax calculation fields
