## WHAT CHANGED & WHY

**Title:**
- Removed "Professional" (filler word, zero search value)
- Added "Job Cost Spreadsheet" and "Bid Proposal Form" (high-intent long-tail keywords)
- Kept both Excel/Google Sheets because buyers filter by compatibility
- Prioritized "for Contractors" early (audience clarity)

**Tags:**
- Mixed broad + specific (Etsy's algorithm rewards both)
- Included "invoice" even though it's not primary—buyers searching for invoices often need estimates
- Added "home improvement estimate" to catch homeowner DIYers
- Avoided redundant plurals (Etsy indexes singular/plural automatically)

**Opening Lines:**
- **Line 1:** Pain point first (slow estimates OR inaccurate pricing)
- **Line 2:** Credibility without bragging ("15-year contractor" > "built by a contractor")
- **Line 3:** Outcome + timeframe (20 min vs 3 hours = tangible value)
- Cut the feature list—moved it down. Hook before specs.

**Reddit Philosophy:**
If I posted this on r/Construction or r/Carpentry, I'd lead with the problem, not the product. "Anyone else spend half their Sunday doing estimates?" That's the energy. Etsy SEO rewards the same thing—search intent matching the buyer's actual frustration.
