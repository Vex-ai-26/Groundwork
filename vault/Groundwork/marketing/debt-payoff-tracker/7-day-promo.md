**Title:** I've been tracking every debt payment in a spreadsheet for 6 months - here's what actually helped

**Copy:**
Started tracking debt payments in January. Not with an app, not with software - just a basic spreadsheet I update every time I make a payment.

What I track:
- Payment date and amount
- Remaining balance after each payment  
- Interest charged that month
- Running total of interest paid (this one hurts)
- Projected payoff date

Why spreadsheet vs. app?

Apps want subscriptions. Apps want to connect to your bank. Apps push notifications I don't need. Spreadsheet sits in my Google Drive, I update it when I make payments, and I can see exactly where my money's going.

The most useful thing? Seeing how extra payments affect the timeline. Threw an extra $200 at a credit card last month. Moved my payoff date up by 3 months and saved $180 in interest. That's a 90% return on money I was going to pay anyway.

Second most useful? Total interest paid tracker. Watched that number hit $847 in February and immediately got more aggressive about payments. Sometimes you need the pain to be visible.

## DAY 5 - REDDIT
**Platform:** r/DaveRamsey  
**Action:** Method discussion with resource
**Title:** Ran the actual numbers on snowball vs. avalanche for my situation - results surprised me

**Copy:**
Dave's snowball method is about behavior change, not math. I get it. Quick wins keep you motivated when you're starting with nothing.

But I wanted to see what the math actually looked like for my situation.

My setup:
- $22,400 total debt across 4 accounts
- Interest rates ranging from 6.9% to 24.99%
- $850/month available for debt payments

**Snowball (smallest to largest):**
- First "win" in 3 months  
- Debt-free in 32 months
- Total interest paid: $8,568

**Avalanche (highest rate first):**
- First payoff in 6 months (slower start)
- Debt-free in 31 months  
- Total interest paid: $4,721

The math says avalanche saves me $3,847. That's almost four months of my current debt payments. That's real money.

But here's the thing - I'm six months into snowball and the momentum is working. I killed two debts already. I'm not sure I would've stayed this aggressive with avalanche.

For people who are spreadsheet-minded and won't lose steam, avalanche is objectively better. For people who need wins to stay motivated, snowball might be worth the premium.
