**Find:** Thread about "How do you track your finances?" or "What financial metrics matter?"

**Copy:**
Net worth tracking changed everything for me.

Most people track income and expenses. That's important, but it's not the whole picture. You can have great income and still be going backward if debt's growing faster than assets.

Net worth = everything you own minus everything you owe. That's the real scorecard.

I track mine monthly. Takes 5 minutes once you've got it set up:

Assets side: checking, savings, investment accounts, retirement, home value, cars, anything else material

Liabilities side: mortgage, car loans, student loans, credit cards, personal loans

The magic is the trend over time. Some months I see:
- Checking looks low, but net worth up $1,200 (paid down debt, 401k contribution hit)
- Checking looks great, but net worth flat (spent the "extra" money instead of investing it)

It's the only number that shows if you're actually building wealth or just moving money around.

I built myself a template that auto-calculates and charts it. Dead simple—just plug in numbers monthly and watch the line. If it trends up, you're winning. If it's flat or down, something needs to change.

Been doing this 18 months. Best financial habit I've picked up.

## DAY 5 - Reddit r/Fire or r/Bogleheads
**Action:** Progress/data post

**Title:** 18 months of net worth tracking data—what I learned

**Copy:**
Started tracking complete net worth monthly in January 2023. Here's what the data showed me.

**The setup:**
Simple spreadsheet. Assets in one column (checking, savings, Roth IRA, 401k, home value, car value). Liabilities in another (mortgage, car loan, one stubborn credit card). Auto-calculates total net worth. First Sunday of every month, 5 minutes to update.

**What I learned:**

Month-to-month income means less than I thought. Had a $4k bonus in month 3. Net worth only went up $1,100 that month because I got loose with spending.

Debt payoff shows up bigger than it feels. Paid extra $200/month on car loan. Felt like nothing. Over 18 months, that's $3,600 gone from liabilities plus interest saved. Shows clearly in the trend.

Market swings are visible but less emotional. My IRA dropped $2k in one month last year. In isolation, that's panic-inducing. On the full net worth chart? Barely a blip because I was still paying down debt and building cash reserves.

The trend is what matters. Individual months bounce around. The 12-month trend line is remarkably steady when you're being consistent. Up and to the right, even if slowly.

**Results after 18 months:**
- Started: $47k net worth
- Now: $68k net worth  
- $21k increase ($1,167/month average)
- Not from income growth—from paying attention to the actual scorecard

I'm not doing anything fancy. No side hustle, no crypto wins, no inheritance. Just tracking the real number and making small adjustments when the trend tells me something's off.

The spreadsheet takes the emotion out of it. It's just data. And data doesn't lie.
